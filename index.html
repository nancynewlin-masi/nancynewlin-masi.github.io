Multi-site harmonization

Diffusion-weighted MRI collected at different imaging sites or with different acquisition protocols exhibit systematic (and unwanted) differences. These differences block us from naively pooling diffusion-weighted images, or derivatives of them, together for joint analysis. I've made progress in "harmonizing" such data, or, making these data comparable between sites/acquisitions.

Stability & robustness in medical imaging

Along the same lines as multi-site harmonization, I have explored the stability and reproducibility models/structures derived from diffusion-weighted images. This includes tractography, connectomes, and graph-measures that summarize brain connectivity. How are these structures effected by the number of samples used to create whole-brain tractography models?

  • Newlin NR, Rheault F, Schilling KG, Landman BA. Characterizing Streamline Count Invariant Graph Measures of Structural Connectomes. J Magn Reson Imaging. 2023 Oct;58(4):1211-1220. doi: 10.1002/jmri.28631. Epub 2023 Feb 24. PMID: 36840398; PMCID: PMC10447626.

Domain-aware learning

I worked independently on a 12 week research project at Microsoft Research (MSR) working with transformers and CT images. The overall motivation was to explore how we can leverage unique features of medical images and enhance attention models. Unlike natural images, a medical image is accompanied by a slew of meta data: what body part is being imaged, the orientation of the body part, and the resolution. This project is a small step toward our overall goal of domain aware learning.
